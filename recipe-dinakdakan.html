Dinakdakan

⏱️ 1 hour 10 minutes
  • • 250g 500g 750g pig’s ears, face, and/or tongue, cleaned well
  • • 1 small 1 medium 1 large onion, chopped
  • • 3 cloves 5-6 cloves 8-9 cloves garlic, crushed
  • • 1 thumb-sized 2 thumb-sized 3 thumb-sized ginger, crushed
  • • 2 pcs 3-4 pcs 5-6 pcs dried bay leaves
  • • 1/2 tsp 1 tsp 1 ½ tsp whole black peppercorns
  • • 2 tbsp 3-4 tbsp 5 tbsp calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • • 1-2 pcs 3-4 pcs 5-6 pcs red chili (siling labuyo), chopped (adjust spice level)
  • • 2 tbsp 4 tbsp 6 tbsp mayonnaise
  • • 1 tsp 2 tsp 1 tbsp fish sauce (optional, for extra saltiness)
  • • to taste to taste to taste salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. 1In a pot, combine the pig parts, garlic, ginger, bay leaves, peppercorns, and a pinch of salt. Add enough water to cover the meat.
  2. 2Bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for 40–50 minutes until the meat is tender but not falling apart. Drain and let cool slightly.
  3. 3Grill or broil the boiled meat until the skin is crispy and slightly charred on the outside. You can also pan-fry if no grill is available.
  4. 4Cut the grilled meat into thin strips or bite-sized pieces and place in a mixing bowl.
  5. 5Add chopped onions, chopped red chilies, calamansi juice, and fish sauce (if using). Toss well to combine.
  6. 6Stir in the mayonnaise and mix until all pieces are evenly coated and the mixture is creamy.
  7. 7Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust calamansi or chili if you want it more sour or spicy.
  8. 8Let it sit for 5–10 minutes to let the flavors blend together.
  9. 9Transfer to a serving plate and serve as an appetizer or main dish, best paired with steamed rice or cold drinks.
