Ginataang Aloy

⏱️ 30 minutes
  • • 250g 500g 750g aloy (squash/kalabasa), peeled and cut into cubes
  • • 1 cup 2 cups 3 cups coconut milk (gata)
  • • 1/2 cup 1 cup 1 ½ cups coconut cream (kakang gata)
  • • 1 small 1 medium 1 large onion, chopped
  • • 2 cloves 3-4 cloves 5-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • • 1 thumb-sized 2 thumb-sized 3 thumb-sized ginger, sliced thinly
  • • 2-3 pcs 4-5 pcs 6-7 pcs green chili (siling haba), sliced (optional)
  • • 1/4 cup 1/2 cup 3/4 cup pork, shrimp, or dried fish (optional)
  • • 1 tbsp 2 tbsp 3 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong) or fish sauce
  • • 1 tbsp 1-2 tbsp 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • • to taste to taste to taste salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. 1Heat cooking oil in a pan or pot over medium heat. Sauté onions until soft and translucent.
  2. 2Add garlic and ginger, then cook until fragrant and lightly golden.
  3. 3If using pork or dried fish, add it now and cook until browned. If using shrimp, set aside to add later.
  4. 4Pour in the coconut milk (gata) and bring to a gentle boil.
  5. 5Add the cubed aloy and mix well. Lower heat, cover, and simmer for 12–15 minutes until the aloy is tender.
  6. 6Stir in shrimp paste or fish sauce and green chilies. Add shrimp now if using.
  7. 7Pour in the coconut cream (kakang gata) and simmer uncovered for another 3–5 minutes until sauce thickens and turns creamy.
  8. 8Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust flavor if needed — add more bagoong or fish sauce for saltiness.
  9. 9Serve hot with steamed white rice.
