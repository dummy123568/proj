Dinuguan

⏱️ 1 hour 10 minutes
  • • 250g 500g 750g pork belly (cubed)
  • • 150g 300g 450g pork innards (liver, intestines, cleaned & cubed) - optional
  • • 1 ½ cups 3 cups 4 ½ cups pork blood (fresh or frozen, strained)
  • • 3 cloves 6 cloves 9 cloves garlic (minced)
  • • 1 small 1 medium 1 large onion (chopped)
  • • 2 pcs 4 pcs 6 pcs green chili peppers (siling haba, sliced)
  • • 1/4 cup 1/2 cup 3/4 cup vinegar
  • • 1 tbsp 2 tbsp 3 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • • 1 cup 2 cups 3 cups water
  • • 1–2 tbsp 2–3 tbsp 3–4 tbsp cooking oil
  • • to taste to taste to taste salt and black pepper
  • • 1 tsp 2 tsp 1 tbsp sugar (optional, to balance flavor)

Instructions

  1. 1Heat oil in a pot or deep pan over medium heat.
  2. 2Sauté garlic and onion until soft and aromatic.
  3. 3Add pork belly (and innards if using) and cook until lightly browned.
  4. 4Pour in water and fish sauce. Bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for 20–30 minutes until meat is tender.
  5. 5Add vinegar — do not stir for 2–3 minutes to let the acid mellow out, then mix gently.
  6. 6Slowly pour in the pork blood while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
  7. 7Add green chili peppers, season with salt, pepper, and sugar (if using). Simmer for another 10–15 minutes until the sauce thickens to your liking.
  8. 8Serve hot with steamed rice — perfect pairing!
