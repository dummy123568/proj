Bicol Express

⏱️ 55 minutes
  • • 250g 500g 750g pork belly, cut into small cubes or strips
  • • 1 cup 2 cups 3 cups coconut milk (gata)
  • • 1/2 cup 1 cup 1 ½ cups coconut cream (kakang gata)
  • • 3-4 pcs 6-8 pcs 9-12 pcs green chili peppers (siling haba), sliced diagonally
  • • 2-3 pcs 4-5 pcs 6-7 pcs red chili peppers (siling labuyo), chopped (adjust for spice level)
  • • 1 small 1 medium 1 large onion, chopped
  • • 3 cloves 5-6 cloves 8-9 cloves garlic, minced
  • • 1 tbsp 2 tbsp 3 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • • 1 tbsp 1-2 tbsp 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • • to taste to taste to taste salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. 1Heat cooking oil in a pan or wok over medium heat. Sauté onions until soft and translucent.
  2. 2Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant and lightly golden.
  3. 3Add the pork pieces and cook until they turn light brown and the fat starts to render.
  4. 4Stir in the shrimp paste (bagoong alamang) and cook for 1–2 minutes to release its flavor.
  5. 5Pour in the coconut milk (gata). Stir well and bring to a gentle boil.
  6. 6Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 25–30 minutes or until the pork is tender and the liquid reduces slightly.
  7. 7Add the green and red chili peppers, then pour in the coconut cream (kakang gata). Stir to combine.
  8. 8Simmer uncovered for another 8–10 minutes until the sauce thickens and turns creamy. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  9. 9Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the saltiness or spice level if needed.
  10. 10Transfer to a serving bowl and serve hot with steamed white rice.
