Ginisang Togue

⏱️ 20 minutes
  • • 2 cups 4 cups 6 cups mung bean sprouts (togue), washed and drained
  • • 1 small 1 medium 1 large onion, chopped
  • • 2 cloves 3-4 cloves 5-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • • 1 small 1 medium 1 large tomato, chopped
  • • 1/4 cup 1/2 cup 3/4 cup carrots, cut into thin strips
  • • 1/4 cup 1/2 cup 3/4 cup green beans, sliced diagonally
  • • 1 tbsp 2 tbsp 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • • 1/2 tbsp 1 tbsp 1 ½ tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • • 1/4 cup 1/2 cup 3/4 cup pork or shrimp (optional, sliced)
  • • 1 tbsp 1-2 tbsp 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • • to taste to taste to taste salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. 1Heat cooking oil in a pan or wok over medium heat. Sauté onions until soft and translucent.
  2. 2Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. 3Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release juice, mashing gently with a spoon.
  4. 4If using pork or shrimp, add them now and cook until the meat turns light brown or shrimp turns pink.
  5. 5Stir in carrots and green beans. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until slightly tender but still crisp.
  6. 6Add the washed mung bean sprouts (togue). Toss gently to mix with the other ingredients.
  7. 7Pour in soy sauce and fish sauce. Stir well and cook for 2-4 minutes only — do not overcook to keep the sprouts crunchy.
  8. 8Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust seasoning if needed.
  9. 9Remove from heat immediately and serve hot with steamed rice.
